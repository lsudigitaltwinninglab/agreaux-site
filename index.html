We build the bridges between tools like Blender, Godot and CloudCompare so they work together, at a fraction of traditional engineering cost.

Capabilities

Everything you need to bring a plant to life

A complete pipeline from raw scan data to an interactive, immersive twin — with the collaboration and analysis features industrial teams actually need.

VR, AR & toy-model modes

Walk the facility at 1:1 scale in VR, shrink it to a tabletop "toy model," or overlay it onto the real equipment in AR passthrough — all from a single Godot project on Meta Quest 3.

Multiplayer with QR anchors

Multiple users share one networked session over ENet. A QR-code spatial anchor pins the model to the same real-world spot for everyone, so co-located users see it aligned in the room.

P&ID with pointer interactions

Pull up Piping & Instrumentation Diagrams in-world and point at hotspots to surface linked metadata and highlight the matching equipment in the 3D model.

Guided modeling from scans

Custom Blender add-ons — Point Cloud Visualizer and Plant Modeling Tools — plus Geometry Nodes turn scaled point clouds into clean, instanced, game-ready geometry fast.

Metadata & process data

Equipment IDs, ratings and contents travel with the model via a metadata manager, ready to display on point-and-click and to extend toward live process-data integration.

Interactive training tools

Move through the unit with walkable ramps and ladder movement, draw annotations in 3D space, and toggle equipment categories — built for safe, repeatable operator training.

Site capture

Scan a whole unit with accessible hardware

You don't need a survey crew or six-figure rig. Agreaux is designed around accessible capture devices and free reconstruction tools, so a small team can digitize a complex process unit and keep the digital model a true 1:1 reference.

  • Low-cost LiDAR scanners — affordable handhelds like the Eagle scanner give an accurate 1:1 laser reference.
  • Photogrammetry — any camera (even a phone) adds high-detail geometry and texture.
  • Registration & export — line up overlapping scans in CloudCompare or RealityScan.
  • Control points — shared reference points keep scale and orientation accurate.

About the project

Game-industry tools, industrial-grade results

Agreaux was built on a simple idea: the entertainment industry has spent decades perfecting fast, cheap workflows for building photoreal interactive worlds — so why not apply them to heavy industry? By pairing consumer hardware with free, open-source software and talent trained in 3D modeling and game development, the framework produces digital twins suitable for industrial visualization, training and review at a fraction of the traditional cost. The reference build digitizes the LSU distillation columns as an accessible, reproducible demo of the whole pipeline.
